Epiphony

February 21, 2018 / by Cristal Fernett

Friday night I finally got Evan to meet me, and it was a strange sort of night. Of course I was early and he was late, but that was ok. He was really nice. I did have to drive very far, but it really wasn't a big deal. We hung out and talked, went for breakfast and talked some more, and spent just as much time sitting in my car talking and not talking. Strangely, it was a great time.

We had arranged to go out again on Monday morning, when he wasn't exhausted. That morning we met up by the Space Needle and went for coffee. After, we drove to Lynnwood to browse at Guitar Center. I was enjoying myself! Then, again, we sat in his truck talking for a long time. We talked about just about everything. He even 'bought me a rock'! Now that's another story!

The next day was extremely tough for me. In the middle of texting Evan, I get a message from Michael. I knew it was comming, but of course I don't want to think about him. He crushed me and I still don't think I'm over that. I'd rather think about Evan, but I'm not sure if his motivations are the same as mine. That worries me.

I had to think a lot yesterday about everything that has happened in my relationships, and the way I handle it. Not well, I think, for the most part. I can't seem to get through the infatuation phase without ruining myself. My actions are complete opposite of what they should be, to get the outcome I want. I know this, but just can't seem to get around it. I really have no patience when it comes to relationships and what I want.

More often than not my emotions consume my thoughts and leave me paralized, unable to move my life forward. I neglect the rest of my life, and the people in it. The problem this time is that I really like Evan...so far...and want it to work out for once in my life!

Maybe if I slow myself down. I should act slowly and take things for what they are. Stop and think about what is happening. Consider what I am doing and why. Consider what the other person is going through and what they are actually doing in response to that. I should stop thinking about what might be and keep my mind in the present. I need to stop reading past what's in front of my eyes; get out of my head for once. If only it were so easy...I need help!
